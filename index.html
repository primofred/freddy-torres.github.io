2020-Present

Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV)
Mechanical Team Member

The organization has the goal of building and upgrading an autonomous underwater vehicle (submarine) for the AUV Competition. The vehicle must complete several tasks with underwater obstacles. Mechanical, Electrical, and Software teams share and apply technical skills with the purpose of gaining design and hands-on experience.

In my time at the AUV team, I have designed and 3Dprinted parts for upgrading areas of the submarine. Moreover, I have been researching for different ways to dissipate heat produced by Electronic Speed Control (ESC), which is a component that regulates the speed of the motor and produces an excesive amount of heat inside the submarine. Using equations of Thermodynamics and Fluid Mechanics, I have been understanding the behavior of the ESCs and analyzing solutions like fans, heat pipes, heat sinks, Peltier coolers, etc.

The team is now in the process of restructuring the submarine. Metal sheets have been cut using a water jet cutter and new designs will come in soon.

Electronic Speed Control (ESC) mount AUV team (Project)

The Electronic Speed Control (ESC) components have the main function of regulating the speed of the motors that run the submarine. One particular issue that the ESCs in the submarine had was that they where hanging with several wires and that produced involuntary damaged when the wires where arragend or other parts where replaced. Another problem of the ESCs is that they produce a great amount of heat. I wanted to find a way to solve the placement issue and improve the heat dissipation.

Using SolidWorks, I designed a mount that could have the ESCs in a fixed posiiton. This way, Electrical Team members could easily replace and manage them. The mounts have an open area and holes that allow heat dissipation, and it has the capability of placing a small fan on top of the ESCs.

After the design was done and revised, a 3DPrinter was used to produce a physical model and it is now used in the submarine.
